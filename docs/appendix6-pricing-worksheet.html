Appendix 6 pricing worksheet (task-based cost build-up)

Appendix 6 ("Payments Based on Tasks Completed") asks for one estimated cost per task
group plus a total. This worksheet is the private build-up behind those four numbers:
roles × hours × loaded rates, plus subconsultant quotes, direct costs, and margin. You keep
this model; only the four task totals and the grand total go onto the official form.

All dollar figures below are ILLUSTRATIVE placeholders to show the method — replace every
rate, hour count, and subconsultant quote with DoChakki's real numbers. The companion file
08_appendix6-pricing-worksheet.csv is the editable spreadsheet version.

Step 1 — Rate card (loaded hourly rates)

"Loaded" = salary + overhead + benefits + profit margin baked into the hourly rate, so you
don't add margin separately. Set these once; every task below pulls from them.

Role Loaded rate (EXAMPLE)
Creative Director $250/hr
Brand Strategist $185/hr
Senior Designer $150/hr
Designer $120/hr
Researcher / Analyst $140/hr
Project / Account Manager $135/hr

Step 4 — Reconcile the 30% M/WBE goal (do this BEFORE finalizing)

The 30% goal is measured against contract value (confirm the exact base via clarification
question 5). Sum every dollar flowing to a NYC-certified M/WBE firm (and DoChakki itself,
only if it's certified and the answer to Q5 allows self-counting), then check it clears 30%.

Item Amount (EXAMPLE)
Total contract price $317,000
Required M/WBE participation (30%) $95,100
Planned M/WBE — Two Twelve (Task 2 $60k + Task 3 $40k) $100,000
Planned M/WBE — [other certified partner, if any] $0
Planned M/WBE total $100,000
% of contract to M/WBE 31.5% ✓

If the planned M/WBE total is below $95,100, either increase the M/WBE subconsultants'
scope/award or add another certified partner (e.g., JPD Studio on the identity side) until
the percentage clears 30%. These award amounts must match the figures you enter in
06_participation-plan-working-sheet.csv (Appendix 4).

Caveats before you submit

  • Pricing is 5% of the score (experience 40%, proposal quality 35%). Be credible and
    defensible    , not cheapest — a lowball that can't sustain the multi-year Task 3 work is a risk.
  • Subconsultant numbers must be real quotes, not guesses — get Two Twelve (and any other
    partner) to put a number to their scope before you lock Tasks 2 and 3.
  • Margin is assumed baked into the loaded rates above. If you prefer to show cost-plus-fee
    instead, strip margin out of the rates and add a single fee line per task.
  • Sales tax is excluded — NYCEDC is tax-exempt (Section IV.B); do not add sales/use tax.
  • Keep this build-up on file — NYCEDC negotiates the Maximum Contract Price from your total, and
    you'll want the underlying hours/rates to defend each figure.
