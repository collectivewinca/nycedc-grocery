B. Work Plan & Approach (≈14 pages)

Address each scope area and how we meet the Aug–Oct 2026 brand-development window so the brand can
feed site design for the end-2027 first-store opening. (No brand concepts — approach only.)

B.1 Brand Research, Strategy & Concepting (Tasks 1A–1D · Jul–Aug 2026)

  • Approach: Pair desktop research and competitive analysis (1A, 1D) with bespoke focus groups and
    observation of City-led community sessions (1B) to ground the brand in real New Yorker behavior at
    the shelf. Build psychographic profiles of grocery shoppers and household decision-makers (1C).
  • NYC-specific lens: study historically significant NYC brands/marks the identity can reference,
    and map loyalty barriers to a new public grocery brand (trust, affordability skepticism, bodega
    loyalty). [DOCHAKKI: name your research methods/tools and a past project where research drove the brand.]
  • Output: an evidence-based positioning platform that the visual identity ladders to.

B.2 Visual Identity & Design Application (Tasks 2A–2C · Aug–Oct 2026)

  • 2A Identity system: brand name, logo (primary/secondary/icon), typography, color, tagline(s) —
    developed as approach and process, not pitched concepts.
  • 2B Environmental application (DoChakki + Two Twelve): guidelines and mockups across the full
    in-store/exterior list — exterior signage, surface finishes, lighting, displays/kiosks, window
    vinyl, wayfinding, aisle/category signage, price labels, private labeling, uniforms, digital
    signage, merch. This is where our brand-to-environment practice shows; Two Twelve hardens the
    wayfinding/signage systems for real fabrication.
  • 2C Iteration: structured feedback rounds with NYCEDC on the selected direction.
  • Consistency engine: a brand guidelines system designed to survive third-party hand-off —
    the bridge into Task 3.

B.3 Architecture & Engineering Collaboration (Tasks 3A–3B · 2027–2029)

  • 3A: participate in per-store brand deployment reviews during design development; produce markups
    and comments on the A&E teams' mid-fitout presentations to keep brand application consistent.
  • 3B: as-needed review of final submittals before signage fabrication, adapting to site-specific
    conditions (window graphics, exterior signage).
  • Why we're credible here: Two Twelve collaborates with architecture/engineering teams as core
    practice — this is the hardest, longest phase, and our team is built for it. (Price against a
    stated review-cycle assumption — see Q8 and the pricing worksheet.)
