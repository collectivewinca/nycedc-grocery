# Win strategy — DoChakki for N.Y.C. Groceries

The internal game plan for *winning* this RFP, not just responding to it. Everything here is
built backward from how NYCEDC actually scores (RFP §VI). Keep this private to the DoChakki team.

## How the award is decided (score weights)

| Weight | Criterion | Where we win it |
|---:|---|---|
| **40%** | Respondent's & team's **experience** delivering similar work | DoChakki's brand-*and*-environmental case studies + Two Twelve's wayfinding/A&E pedigree |
| **35%** | **Proposal quality** — understanding of the work, schedule, and budget | The work-plan narrative: a credible, NYC-specific approach per task |
| **15%** | **M/WBE participation** plan | Certified partners integral to scope, ≥30%, not bolted on |
| **5%** | Management, reputation, **references** | 3+ strong civic/retail references; clear org structure |
| **5%** | **Fee/cost** schedule | Credible, defensible Appendix 6 — not lowball |

**Implication:** 75% of the score is experience + proposal quality. Win those two and the bid is
ours; the rest is table stakes we must not fumble.

## Positioning statement (the one sentence everything ladders to)

> **DoChakki builds brand identities that become real places** — we design the mark *and* the
> environment it lives in, then carry that brand intact from concept through construction.
> For N.Y.C. Groceries, that means a brand New Yorkers trust at the shelf, deployed consistently
> across five boroughs, with a certified M/WBE team that de-risks the 2027–2029 build-out.

This directly answers the RFP's two Respondent-Profile requirements: (1) galvanizing brand
identities *and* their physical extensions in immersive, place-based environments, and
(2) collaborating with architecture & engineering firms.

## Win themes (thread these through every section of the proposal)

1. **"Identity that becomes architecture."** Our edge is the seam most firms can't cross — brand
   strategy *through* built environment. Big brand agencies hand off a logo; environmental firms
   lack brand thinking. DoChakki owns the whole arc. *Proof:* `[DOCHAKKI: 2 case studies where you
   took an identity into physical/retail/place-based space — name, scope, outcome, image]`.

2. **"Built for civic trust and food access."** N.Y.C. Groceries is a *public* affordability
   mission, not a retail launch. We design for legibility, dignity, and trust across diverse
   neighborhoods — not aspirational lifestyle branding. *Proof:* `[DOCHAKKI: any civic / public /
   community / mission-driven work; multilingual or accessibility-minded design]`.

3. **"A team that survives the hand-off to construction (2027–2029)."** Brands usually degrade
   when architects and fabricators take over. Two Twelve's wayfinding + A&E-collaboration pedigree
   (MTA, Brooklyn Public Library, cultural institutions) guarantees the brand standards hold through
   deployment reviews and signage fabrication. This is Task 3 insurance.

4. **"M/WBE by design."** Our 30%+ participation flows to certified partners doing *core* scope
   (Two Twelve on environmental/wayfinding), not token roles — credible and defensible.

## Who we're likely beating, and how

| Competitor type | Their weakness | Our counter |
|---|---|---|
| Large brand agencies (e.g., Carbone Smolan tier) | Strong identity, weak built-environment + thin/none M/WBE | We own environmental + a real M/WBE team |
| Environmental graphics / wayfinding firms | Execute signage, but no brand *strategy* or research depth | We lead with research + strategy, then deploy |
| Generalist design shops | No civic/food-equity insight, no A&E collaboration proof | Civic-trust theme + Two Twelve A&E pedigree |
